Small Engine Dynamometer

Fusion360 3D Printing Engineering Design Manual Machining

Project Overview

This project focuses on designing and building an affordable, portable, and environmentally responsible dynamometer for small displacement engine testing.

Developed as part of a senior mechanical engineering design course, I collaborated with 4 others to develop a prototype that combines hydraulic loading, real-time data acquisition, and exhaust filtration into a single, compact platform.

The Challenge

Small displacement engine builders often lack access to dynamometer testing due to the high cost, large footprint, and environmental limitations of existing solutions. Traditional dynos are typically expensive, occupy significant space, and do not include exhaust filtration, making indoor testing difficult or unsafe. This is especially problematic for two-stroke and high-RPM engines, where improper tuning can lead to catastrophic failure within minutes of operation.

The engineering requirements for this problem included:

  • Safely withstand high RPMs and elevated exhaust temperatures
  • Apply controlled load to the engine for performance measurement
  • Capture accurate torque and horsepower data
  • Filter exhaust gases to environmentally safe levels
  • Remain portable, stable, and manufacturable within a constrained budget

The Solution

The Green Go-Kart Dyno integrates engine performance measurement with an on-board exhaust filtration system, enabling safer indoor testing without the need for external ventilation.

The solution uses a hydraulic pressure-based dynamometer to apply load to the engine, allowing torque and horsepower to be calculated from pressure, flow rate, and RPM data. Engine speed is measured using a proximity sensor, while pressure data is collected through a hydraulic pressure sensor. An Arduino-based data acquisition system records sensor data in real time and exports it for post-processing and visualization.

To address environmental and safety concerns, the dyno incorporates a built-in exhaust filtration system. Exhaust gases are routed through snaked ducting, actively cooled with fans, and passed through high-efficiency filters before being released. This design reduces exhaust temperature and pollutant concentration, enabling safer operation in enclosed environments.

My Role & Contributions

Within our five-member senior design team, I took a leading role in the mechanical design and system integration of the Green Go-Kart Dyno. I was primarily responsible for translating early concepts into manufacturable designs by modeling the prototype and custom components in Fusion 360. This included designing parts to proper tolerances, validating fit and alignment, and ensuring compatibility with purchased hardware prior to fabrication.
